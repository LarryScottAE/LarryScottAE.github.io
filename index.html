Wikipedia MIDI Definition
Click on this arrow for very basic Midi overview


MIDI (Musical Instrument Digital Interface) is a technical standard (communication protocol) that allows musical devices (instruments, computers, recording software, pedalboards, effects processors, and more) to communicate with each other. Midi is used to communicate music data, music commands, and vendor specific (system exclusive) commands:

Music Data:, Midi is used to record and play musical note information. For example, when you press a note on a digital piano keyboard, the note number (ie: note pressed) and the note velocity (how hard the note was played) is encoded to digital Midi data which is sent to your computer's recording (DAW) software. Software such as Pro Tools, Reaper, Cubase, can read, record, and play back midi data. Playback of a midi recorded keyboard performance will sound identical to the original performance. Midi music data implementation is standardized across all music gear manufacturers.

Music Commands:Your digital piano keyboard likely has button, sliders, foot pedals, and wheels. In Midi jargon, these are called ''continuous controllers', or more commonly, 'controllers'.. Controllers do not change the note being played but rather transmit information to the listening device. For example, your keyboard may have a slider that is use to control the volume of your keyboard. This slider does not affect the note being played, but 'controls' (the volume) of the note being played by sending a Midi 'continuous controller 7' information to your PC, amplifier, or PA system. The target device recognizes the volume controller and adjusts the keyboard volume accordingly. Midi Continuous Controller implementation is standardized across all music gear manufacturers.

Vendor Specific Commands, Midi provides a free form Midi format called 'System Exclusive' which provides musical equipment vendors with options to implement manufacturer specific functions in their musical equipment. For example, you may own a Roland guitar effects processor that has hundreds of 'patches'. Each patch has many possible settings (reverb, chorus, delay,...). Roland guitar effects processors have unique internal circuitry, so Roland implements unique SysEx commands to save and restore a backup to your PC. Midi System Exclusive command are vendor specific, therefore, NOT standardized across all music gear manufacturers.


MidiXLate Midi Router
Midi Routing is the process of transmitting and receiving midi packets (digitally encoded music notes and control messages) from one musical device to another. Typically, Midi Routing is accomplished by connecting all Midi devices to a midi 'hub' (or 'switch') and 'broadcasting' all midi data to all midi devices connected to the midi hub (or switch). This is technically not routing because midi commands are 'broadcast', not 'routed' to their intended destination. There is no intelligence involved (ie: looking at every midi packet and making a rules based decision on where to send the midi packet) Sending all Midi data to all midi devices is obviously not optimal because it generates excessive midi network traffic and makes each Midi device configuration overly complicated ('filtering' the midi data intended for each midi device).

The MidiXLate router is a true router, meaning every midi packet is inspected and 'routed' to it's intended destinations. Routing Midi data greatly reduces Midi network traffic, eliminating a flood of extraneous midi network data. The sending and receiving midi devices are now much simpler to configure because the midi data received at a midi device is guaranteed to be intended solely for that midi device. For the average home user with only a few Midi devices, a Midi hub is the right option. For a performing group (or sophisticated home studio) with a large number of midi devices and complicated midi network, the MidiXLate router can be a life saver; not only due to MidiXLate's routing capability, but also MidiXlate's 'translation' and 'channel locking' capabilities (discussed below) Setting up the MidiXLate router is obviously more complicated than setting up a dumb Midi hub, but MidiXLate's benefits can be huge. With MidiXLate, you have complete control of Midi routing and port filtering (note, controller, patch change, and sys ex) capabilities. As for MidiXLate's performance, the engine of the router is a blinding fast Atmel SAM3X8E ARM Cortex-M3 processor running at 84 MHz, providing extremely low latency.

In addition to Midi routing, the MidiXLate router provides a sudo Network Address Translation (NAT) features called 'Translation' where midi data can be 'translated from one Midi format (such as note data) to another Midi format (such as continuous controller). This is extremely helpful because it allows you to extract maximum usability out of your music gear (by providing useful access to previously inaccessible controllers). Next, MidiXLate has a Channel Locking feature providing flexible rules to lock midi data (sent out an output port) to a particular midi channel. For example, assume you have a drum machine assigned to output port 6. All drum patches are required to be encoded to Midi channel 10, yet some drum patch providers fail to encode their drum patches to midi channel 10. With MidiXLate, you can force all data to your drum machine to midi port 10. Channel Locking has many other useful features.


The MidiXLate router was the created in response to many of my home studio music production issues. MidiControl Software (for Windows 10 and above)     FREE!

Have you every wanted to catalog and control all of your midi devices from your Windows PC?   MidiControl provides a FREE Windows PC program to control all of your midi devices from your PC desktop, using Midi 'program change','continuous control', and 'system exclusive' commands. MidiControl is not designed to play midi files, rather to control midi devices from your desktop. MidiControl provides ten bookmarks covering 2000 midi commands.

You, a typical musician, likely have many midi devices such as keyboards, guitar effects, drum machines, voice processors, and more. You may find it helpful to catalog all of your midi commands and send those commands from the PC desktop, just as they are sent from your midi device. For example, assume you have a performance where every song requires a different keyboad patch, drum pathch, guitar patch, guitar volume, .... With MidiControl, you can define groups (tabs) by song and list all the midi commands for each midi device to setup the song. Using your PC desktop, you can send commands to all your midi devices to setup for the next song (patch changes, controllers, ...) Alternatively, as shown in the example screens, each tab can represent a musical device and the midi commands to control the device.

To start, connect all of your midi devices to your computer via a Midi hub (which you have likely already have done). Next, download, install, and configure MidiControl (FREE!). When configuring midi control, define groups ('tabs') for each midi device and/or song and then setup the same midi commands used by your midi gear (keyboards, pedal boards, effects processors,...) You now have a simple means to control all of your Midi devices from your PC!

MidiOrganize Software (for Windows 10 and above)     FREE!

MidiOrganize provides a FREE and simple means to organize your midi drum loops and optimize the performance and flexibility of your DAW mixes! MidiOrganize is a PC midi file organizer (not a VST) designed to catalog midi drum loops and midi (guitar, bass, horn,...) patches. There are many license based drum loop VSTs available for ProTools, Reaper, Cubase,... Most drum VSTs typically are very expensive, must be licensed and require your PC to be connected to the internet, and (in my case, the most irritating issue) these licensed VSTs place a heavy burden on the CPU introducing latency and popping in my final mix. MidiOrganize is a FREE standalone PC application. You may choose to download Groove Monkee's excellent FREE drumloops and easily configure for use with MidiOrganize. Additionally, you can use MidiOrganize with any of your purchased drum loops.

My PC rack computer is a music optimized PC (32 gig memory, 1TB HD, Intel 7 CPU, Reaper). I typically start to see performance degradation after only loading a few drum VSTs. VSTs do have benefits and conveniences, but I prefer to limit my VST usage to optimize my performance and maximize patch selection flexibilty during playback. Midi Organize is a stand alone PC application. During setup, you specify the directories of all your drum loops. When working with your DAW (ProTools, Reaper, CuBase,...) you drag and drop MidiOrganize drum tracks into your DAW drum tack. Unlike the VST which will play the drum track, set your drum track to route the drum midi to your external drum machine. In my case, I have a Yamaha MU15 and a Roland Integra7. The MU15 (or Integra7) audio output is an input on my mixer which is in turn recorded by my DAW. Not only is this a FREE solution, but the PC CPU performance hit on your DAW is significantly reduced. In my case, with Reaper, the final mix rendered audio is free of chop, pops, and dropouts due to heavy VST memory usage. MidiOrganize follows the format of EZ Drummer. If you understand the drilldown structure of EZ Drummer, MidiOrganize will feel very natural.

Midi Translations
Midi Translations are used to change a Midi message
from one format to another. For Example: assume you
want to press a buttton (controller) on you keyboard
to send a 'patch change' command to your tone generator.
Use the translation screen to identify and capture the
midi CC (continuous control) command and translate it
to a Midi PC (program change) cmd.

Translation Screen

  • 256 midi translations are permitted.
  • Copy, paste, and delete functions are provided.
  • Bulk copy, paste, and delete functions are provided.
Midi Channel Locks
Midi Channel Locks are used to lock specific Midi data
to a specific midi channel (by Midi port and Midi command).
For example, assume you have a Reaper MIDI DAW track that
contains a drum patch data routed to your Yamaha MU15 drum
machine. Some rougue drum tracks do not encode drum notes
to Midi channel 10. Use the channel lock screen to guarantee
all Reaper Midi drum data sent to your Yamaha Mu15 is locked
to Midi channel 10.

Channel Lock Screen
  • Specify the input and output ports.
  • Specify the channel to lock outbound midi data.
  • Specify if the lock is CC,PC,NT,or all midi data.
  • 96 midi locks are permitted.
  • Copy, paste, and delete functions are provided.
  • Bulk copy, paste, and delete functions are provided.
